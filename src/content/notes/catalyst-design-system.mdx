#### Context
## Three products merged. <br />Zero consistency.
 
[In early 2022 Haiilo was formed](https://blog.haiilo.com/blog/smarp-coyo-merger/) by merging COYO, Smarp, and Jubiwee. Each company brought in their expertise: Social intranet, Employee advocacy and internal surveys.
The goal was simple: Combine them into a single high quality employee engagement suite. 

The reality however was messy. Three products, three design languages, three tech stacks. Put them side by side and they looked like competitors, not a coherent platform.
 

---
 
 
Customers and prospects picked this up and were concerned about the products they already bought. Will this impact their own plattform and users engangement? Others questioned whether Haiilo actually had a solid product strategy.

Sales worried it would kill deals. <br />
Product teams worried it would slow down development.
 
---
 
#### The goal
## Position Haiilo as one company with one vision, not three products duct-taped together.



#### Objectives

* Support G2M by telling a cohesive product story through design.
* Keep up momentum by enabling teams to build faster, not slower.
* Soften internal culture shock by signaling unity through a shared design language.
 


#### Business bets
 
1. **A unified look** will help customers understand Haiilo's value.
If everything looks like one product, customers will treat it like one product.

2.  **A robust system** will speed up development.  
Reusable components mean teams stop rebuilding the same things three different ways.
 
3. **Plug-and-play components** will make teams self-sufficient.  
If it's easy to use, teams won't need constant design support.
 
4. **A shared system** breaks down silos.  
Merging teams is hard. A shared design language makes it easier.
